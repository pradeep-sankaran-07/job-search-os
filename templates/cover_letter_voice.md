# Cover-letter template

This is the structure every cover letter follows. The onboarding skill stores your personal voice snippet in `profile.yaml` under `voice_snippet`, and the daily skill uses it as a style anchor. You can edit this file to change the structure for your letters.

## Length

150–180 words, 3–4 paragraphs. One page, always.

## Three-move structure

### Move 1 — Opening insight (spend 50% of your effort here)

Identify the specific tension, inflection point, or non-obvious challenge at this company RIGHT NOW. What makes this role different from 20 similar roles at competitors?

Open with that insight — NOT with "I am writing to apply" or any self-introduction.

### Move 2 — Evidence mapped to THEIR challenge

Pick 1–2 specific experiences from your background that directly parallel the role's challenge. Include at least one concrete metric or outcome. Do NOT list your whole career — only the pieces that rhyme with their problem.

### Move 3 — Forward-looking strategic question

Close with a genuine question about their product/strategy or a forward-looking observation about where the company is headed. This signals you've thought about their future, not just your next job.

Then: a short warm close ("Happy to connect.", "Would love to talk.").

## Voice rules

**Do:**
- Short punchy open, longer evidence paragraph, sharp close
- Em-dashes for rhythm and emphasis (if your voice uses them)
- Match the tone of your `voice_snippet` in `profile.yaml`

**Do NOT use:**
- "Proven track record"
- "Unique blend of"
- "I am writing to apply"
- "I look forward to hearing from you"
- Chronological career summary
- Anything that sounds like a template

Tone should be confident but not arrogant — "That is the kind of hard that I find interesting" not "I am the best person for this".

## Example skeleton (fill in based on a real role)

> [Company] is [specific observation about their current inflection point or product challenge]. That is the kind of problem I have spent the last [N] years working on.
>
> At [your last company], I [specific achievement relevant to their challenge] — [concrete metric], [timeframe], [scope].
>
> [Forward-looking question or observation about where they're headed.]
>
> Happy to connect.
>
> [Your name]
> [City, Country] | [email] | [phone]

## LinkedIn outreach draft (companion to cover letter)

For every Strong-fit role, also draft a 3–4 sentence LinkedIn note to send to a hiring manager, CPO, or product director at the company. LinkedIn's note character limit is ~300.

**Structure:**
- Opens with a genuine observation about their product/company — NOT "I saw you're hiring"
- One specific hook connecting your background to their challenge
- Low-friction ask: "Happy to connect" or "Would love to hear your perspective on [specific product question]"

Never paste the cover letter into LinkedIn — this is a human conversation starter.

If the daily skill can't identify a specific person at the company, it writes the message with `[Name]` as a placeholder and adds: `"Find: CPO or Head of Product on LinkedIn before sending."`
